The Shift Nobody Named
From "grab users at any cost" to "which use of this GPU-hour returns the most?"
Marginal-Cost Era
"How do we acquire more users?"
Marginal cost per user: ~$0

Strategy: Grab users now, monetize later

Constraint: Distribution and attention
→
Opportunity-Cost Era
"What returns the most per GPU-hour?"
Marginal cost per user: $1.50 to $7/hr

Strategy: Allocate compute to highest-value use

Constraint: GPU capacity and energy

The Sora Pattern: From Viral Launch to Shutdown in Under a Year
What happens when a pure renter's product scales faster than its rented capacity
Feb 2025
Sora launches
OpenAI releases its video generation app to broad consumer access
Oct 2025
API token usage: 6B per minute
Compute demand is growing but still manageable across the fleet
Nov 2025
"Our GPUs are melting"
Head of Sora rations free users to 6 videos/day. Compute is scarce.
Mar 2026
API token usage: 15B per minute
2.5x increase in 5 months. CFO spends most of her time hunting capacity.
Apr 26, 2026
Sora shuts down
Rented compute redirected to coding agents and enterprise APIs (codename "Spud")
If your vendor ships a consumer product on your infrastructure, watch for this pattern
